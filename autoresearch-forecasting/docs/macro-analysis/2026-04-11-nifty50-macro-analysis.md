## Macro Regime Narrative

### Regime: Late Cycle with Stagflation Risk — Moderate Confidence

India's economy is transitioning from a high-growth phase (FY26 at 7.6%) into a decelerating trajectory (FY27 at 6.9%) under the shadow of a severe exogenous energy shock. The domestic fundamentals — fiscal discipline, healthy bank balance sheets, infrastructure spending — remain structurally sound. But the US-Iran war and Strait of Hormuz disruption have injected a stagflationary impulse that is compressing margins, elevating input costs, weakening the rupee, and draining foreign portfolio capital. This is not yet a recession, but the economy is clearly past its cyclical peak and navigating a minefield of external risks.

### Dominant Force

**The energy shock from the US-Iran war is the single most important macro dynamic.** Crude oil at $108/bbl (up 57% from February) with projections up to $175/bbl in a worst case, combined with the Strait of Hormuz disruption, is the defining variable for India's near-term macro trajectory. India imports 90% of its crude — every $10/bbl increase widens the CAD by ~0.4% of GDP and adds ~30-40 bps to headline inflation. The rupee has weakened ~9% since early 2025, amplifying the inflationary pass-through. This single force explains the RBI's rate hold (despite earlier easing bias), the FII exodus, the PMI deceleration, and the corporate bond market stress.

### Supporting Forces

- **Growth deceleration is broadening.** Manufacturing PMI at 53.9 (4-year low), Services PMI at 57.5 (14-month low), and Composite PMI at 57.0 all point to slowing momentum. Morgan Stanley expects Q1 FY27 growth as low as 5.9%.
- **FII capital flight is persistent.** ₹1.79 lakh crore sold in FY26, with ₹48,213 crore sold in April alone. FPIs are redirecting to South Korea and Taiwan for superior earnings growth.
- **Corporate bond market stress.** AAA spreads widened to 181 bps (1Y), issuance dropped 6% YoY, and corporates are shifting to bank credit — a sign of tightening financial conditions.
- **Consumer credit stress emerging.** Credit card NPAs up 45% YoY to ₹6,742 crore, unpaid dues nearly doubled to ₹34,000 crore. Unsecured lending is showing cracks.
- **Rupee depreciation amplifying imported inflation.** At ~94/USD, down 9% from early 2025, the rupee is acting as an inflation amplifier rather than shock absorber (RBI estimates 5% depreciation adds ~35 bps to inflation when layered on energy shocks).

### Counter-Signals

- **Headline CPI remains benign at 3.21%** — well within RBI's 4% target. Core inflation at 3.4% shows no broad-based domestic price pressure yet. The stagflation risk is prospective, not current.
- **Fiscal policy remains supportive.** Capex at ₹12.2L crore (+11.5%) provides a structural floor for infrastructure and capital goods demand. The government is not withdrawing stimulus.
- **Bank balance sheets are the healthiest in a decade.** GNPAs at 2.3-2.5% (near historic lows), credit growth at 13-14%, and banks are well-capitalized to absorb moderate stress.
- **Services sector remains resilient.** Export orders hit near-record levels, employment is expanding, and business confidence is at a 12-year high despite the PMI pullback.
- **US-India trade deal reduced tariffs from 50% to 18%**, partially restoring export competitiveness and removing a structural overhang.
- **India has 60 days of petroleum reserves**, providing near-term buffer against supply disruptions.
- **Retail sales grew 9% YoY** in February, and capital goods production surged 12.5%, suggesting investment cycle remains intact.

### Key Inflection Risks

1. **US-Iran peace talks (this weekend, April 11-12):** De-escalation → crude falls below $90 → regime shifts to Goldilocks/disinflationary boom. Failed talks → crude spikes toward $130-175 → full stagflation.
2. **RBI's next move:** If inflation breaches 5% persistently, the RBI may be forced to hike rates, choking growth. Currently on hold at 5.25% with an upward inflation revision to 4.6%.
3. **Monsoon quality (June-September 2026):** A poor monsoon would spike food inflation and rural distress, compounding the energy shock. A normal monsoon would ease food prices and support rural demand.
4. **FII flow reversal:** If geopolitical tensions ease and earnings growth revives, India's underperformance could trigger FII re-entry. Continued selling would pressure the rupee further.
5. **US-India trade deal implementation:** The $500B goods purchase commitment over 5 years is vaguely defined. Implementation delays could reignite tariff tensions.

## Dimension Deep-Dives

### 1. Monetary Policy

- **Current state:** RBI repo rate at 5.25% (unchanged April 8, 2026). Second consecutive hold after pausing in February. Neutral stance maintained.
- **Direction:** Pause (was easing, now on hold)
- **Key data points:**
  - Repo rate: 5.25%; SDF: 5.00%; MSF: 5.50% (Apr 8, 2026)
  - ₹2.15 lakh crore liquidity injected Jan-Feb 2026 via OMOs, USD/INR swaps, VRR auctions
  - TREPS rate fell 75 bps below SDF — banks parking ₹5L crore with RBI rather than lending (liquidity trap signal)
  - 10Y G-sec yield: 6.93% (Mar 27) → expected 7.25% by April end
  - Government borrowing ₹8.2 trillion in H1 FY27 adding supply pressure
  - FY27 CPI projection revised up to 4.6% (from 4.5%)
  - FY27 GDP projection: 6.9%
- **What to watch next:** June 2026 MPC meeting. If crude stays above $100 and inflation breaches 4.5%, the RBI may shift stance to "withdrawal of accommodation" or signal rate hikes. Watch for any CRR/SLR adjustments.

### 2. Fiscal Policy

- **Current state:** Budget FY27 targets 4.3% fiscal deficit (from 4.4% in FY26). Total expenditure ₹53.47L crore.
- **Direction:** Mildly expansionary (capex up, but pace of consolidation slowing)
- **Key data points:**
  - Capital expenditure: ₹12.22L crore (3.1% of GDP, +11.5% YoY)
  - Effective capex (incl. state loans): ₹17.1L crore (4.36% of GDP)
  - Tax revenue target: ₹28.67L crore (+7.2% YoY)
  - Debt-to-GDP target: 55.6% (from 56.1%)
  - Infrastructure Risk Guarantee Fund established to attract private capital
  - PLI allocation: ₹15,541 crore (automobiles tripled to ₹5,940 crore)
  - Gross tax-to-GDP ratio declining: 11.5% → 11.2%
- **What to watch next:** GST collection trends (monthly indicator of economic health). Any mid-year spending cuts if oil shock deteriorates fiscal math. Subsidy burden from crude — fertilizer and LPG subsidies could balloon.

### 3. Inflation & Pricing

- **Current state:** CPI at 3.21% (Feb 2026) — well within RBI's 4% target. Core inflation stable at 3.4%. But energy shock threatens to push headline above 5%.
- **Direction:** Currently benign but accelerating risk from energy
- **Key data points:**
  - CPI Feb 2026: 3.21% (up from 2.74% in Jan)
  - Core CPI: 3.4% (Feb 2026) — no broad-based pressure
  - Food & beverages inflation: 3.47% — volatile (tomatoes +45%, onions -28%)
  - Crude oil basket: $108.23/bbl (Mar 2026) — up 57% from February
  - Rupee: ~94/USD — 9% depreciation since early 2025
  - RBI FY27 CPI projection: 4.6% (upward revision)
  - CEA warning: $130/bbl crude → CPI could spike to 5.5%
  - Manufacturing input costs at steepest since Aug 2022
  - Services input costs at 45-month high
- **What to watch next:** April CPI print (mid-May release), crude oil trajectory post peace talks, LPG price adjustments, monsoon impact on food prices.

### 4. Labor Market

- **Current state:** Stable but not showing acceleration. Unemployment at 4.9% — near cyclical lows.
- **Direction:** Stable with mild improvement
- **Key data points:**
  - Unemployment rate: 4.9% (Feb 2026) — down from 5.0% in Jan
  - Urban unemployment: 6.6%; Rural: 4.2%
  - Female unemployment improved: 5.1% (from 5.6%)
  - LFPR: 55.9% (unchanged)
  - Worker Population Ratio: 53.1% (minor contraction due to seasonal rural slowdown)
  - EPFO: 1.29 crore net subscribers added in 2024-25; 60% in 18-25 age group
  - Services PMI employment expanding for 3rd consecutive month at strongest pace since Jun 2025
- **What to watch next:** PLFS monthly data, EPFO payroll trends, any large-scale layoffs in IT services sector (exposure to global demand). Wage growth data remains a gap.

### 5. Growth & Output

- **Current state:** High growth (FY26 at 7.6%) but clearly decelerating. Broad-based slowdown across manufacturing and services.
- **Direction:** Decelerating
- **Key data points:**
  - GDP FY26: 7.6% (new series); Q3 FY26: 7.8% (down from 8.4% Q2)
  - RBI FY27 GDP forecast: 6.9% (Q1: 6.8%, Q2: 6.7%, Q3: 7.0%, Q4: 7.2%)
  - Morgan Stanley Q1 FY27 estimate: 5.9%
  - Manufacturing PMI: 53.9 (Mar 2026) — 4-year low
  - Services PMI: 57.5 (Mar 2026) — 14-month low
  - Composite PMI: 57.0 — lowest since Nov 2022
  - IIP: 5.2% (Feb 2026); Capital goods: +12.5%; Consumer non-durables: -0.6%
  - Retail sales: +9% YoY (Feb 2026)
  - Export orders at near-record levels despite domestic demand weakening
- **What to watch next:** Q4 FY26 GDP (May/June release), April PMI flash estimates, auto sales data for April (early bellwether).

### 6. Credit Conditions

- **Current state:** Bank credit flowing but financial conditions tightening. Unsecured lending stress emerging. Corporate bond market under pressure.
- **Direction:** Gradual tightening
- **Key data points:**
  - Bank credit growth: 13-14% FY27 estimate (up from 11% FY25)
  - GNPA: 2.3-2.5% (near historic lows, likely bottomed)
  - NBFC AUM growth: 18-19%, crossing ₹50L crore by Mar 2027
  - Credit card NPAs: 2.30% (up from 1.84%), defaults up 45% YoY to ₹6,742 crore
  - Unpaid credit card dues: ₹34,000 crore (from ₹23,000 crore)
  - Auto loan delinquency (PAR 31-180): 2.8% (Sep 2025)
  - AAA corporate bond spreads: 181 bps (1Y), 91 bps (3Y) — widening
  - Corporate bond issuance: -6% YoY in 9M FY26
  - Secured lending grew 42% YoY — migration from unsecured to secured
  - Net 30+ delinquency improved: 3.9% → 3.3% (overall market)
- **What to watch next:** RBI Financial Stability Report (June), SLOOS equivalent data, NBFC asset quality in Q4 results, corporate default rates if crude stays elevated.

### 7. Geopolitical & Trade

- **Current state:** Severe exogenous shock. US-Iran war has disrupted Strait of Hormuz, crude above $100, but US-India trade deal partially offsets tariff risk.
- **Key developments:**
  - US-Iran war began Feb 28, 2026; Strait of Hormuz partially blocked
  - Crude oil basket: $108.23/bbl (Mar 2026); worst-case projections up to $175/bbl
  - India secured 60 days of oil supply; diversified to 41+ suppliers
  - Domestic LPG production increased 40-50%
  - US-India trade deal (Feb 2, 2026): tariffs cut from 50% to 18%
  - India committed to stop buying Russian oil; purchase $500B US goods over 5 years
  - FIIs sold ₹1.79L crore in FY26; ₹48,213 crore in April alone
  - Forex reserves: $697.12B (Apr 3) — down from $728.49B peak (Feb 27)
  - Rupee: ~94/USD (9% weaker since early 2025)
- **Risk scenarios:**
  - **Best case:** US-Iran peace deal → crude drops to $70-80 → massive relief rally, rupee strengthens, FIIs return, RBI resumes easing
  - **Base case:** Protracted conflict, crude $90-110 → managed slowdown, inflation 4.5-5%, growth 6.5-7%
  - **Worst case:** Full Hormuz closure, crude $130-175 → stagflation, GDP 5.5-6%, CPI above 6%, rate hikes, deep market correction

## Regime Signal Summary

The Indian macro environment as of April 11, 2026 is best characterized as **Late Cycle with Stagflation Risk**:

**What makes it Late Cycle:**
- Growth decelerating from peak (7.6% → 6.9%)
- PMIs falling but still above 50
- Corporate bond spreads widening
- FII capital exiting
- Credit conditions gradually tightening

**What makes Stagflation a risk (but not yet reality):**
- External energy shock ($108/bbl crude)
- Rupee depreciation amplifying imported inflation
- Input costs at multi-year highs
- RBI forced to pause easing despite growth deceleration

**What keeps it from being full Stagflation:**
- Headline CPI still at 3.21% (well below target)
- Core inflation benign at 3.4%
- Fiscal policy remains stimulative
- Bank balance sheets healthy
