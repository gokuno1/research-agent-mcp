## Phase 4: Macro Regime Synthesis

### Regime: **STAGFLATIONARY OVERHANG WITH DOMESTIC RESILIENCE** (Late Cycle)

**Confidence:** Moderate

This is NOT a clean stagflation — India's domestic economy is fundamentally healthy (6.9% GDP, record auto sales, low unemployment). But the **exogenous oil shock** from the Iran war is imposing stagflationary pressure on an otherwise sound economy. The key question is duration: if crude stays at $120+ for 3-6 months, the macro picture deteriorates materially. If the ceasefire holds and Hormuz reopens, India reverts to a growth-friendly environment.

### Dominant Macro Force

**Crude oil / Iran war shock** — Brent at $120-128/bbl (vs $70 pre-war) is the single most important variable. India imports ~85% of its crude. Every $10/bbl rise:
- Widens current account deficit by ~0.4% of GDP
- Adds ~30-40 bps to headline CPI within 2-3 months
- Forces RBI to pause/reverse easing cycle
- Weakens INR (already at ₹93+/USD)
- Triggers FPI outflows (₹1.8L Cr YTD and counting)

### Supporting Forces (Negative)

1. **FPI exodus** — ₹1.8 lakh crore outflows in CY2026, record ₹1.17L Cr in March alone. FPIs selling into relief rallies.
2. **Rupee depreciation** — INR at ~93/USD, adding imported inflation and reducing FII returns.
3. **PMI deceleration** — Manufacturing PMI at 53.9 (near-4-year low), services sector at 14-month slow. Growth momentum fading.
4. **Corporate bond spreads widening** — AAA 1Y spreads at 181 bps (from 151), signaling tightening financial conditions.
5. **IT sector weakness** — Nifty IT down 20% in 3 months, FY27 guidance cautious (3-5% growth), AI disruption concerns.

### Supporting Forces (Positive — Domestic Resilience)

1. **Inflation well-contained** — CPI at 3.40%, well below RBI's 4% target. Provides policy headroom.
2. **RBI has ammunition** — Repo at 5.25% with 125 bps already cut; can cut further if growth falters. Surplus liquidity (₹5.14L Cr).
3. **Fiscal impulse strong** — Capex at ₹12.2L Cr (4.4% GDP), highest in a decade. Infrastructure spending is a direct GDP multiplier.
4. **Consumer demand healthy** — Record auto sales (2.97 Cr units FY26, +13.3%), rural demand outpacing urban.
5. **Labor market tight** — 4.9% unemployment, workers leaving MGNREGA for better-paying formal jobs.
6. **Bank credit flowing** — 13-14% growth, NPAs declining, no systemic stress visible.
7. **Steel / metals protected** — Anti-dumping duties on Chinese steel, domestic demand growing 8% annually.
8. **India-US trade deal** — Tariffs reduced from 50% to 18%, $15B smartphone export opportunity.

### Counter-Signals (What Contradicts the Bearish Case)

- CPI at 3.40% is actually disinflationary — the oil shock hasn't passed through yet
- Auto sales and consumer spending show NO demand destruction yet
- Bank credit is accelerating, not contracting
- Fiscal spending is counter-cyclical and will support GDP even if private demand falters
- The ceasefire, while fragile, is holding — Trump says "very close to over"
